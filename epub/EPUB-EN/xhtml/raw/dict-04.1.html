The concept of innovative management

  • Innovation management

    is the activity of administering of innovations (novelties) - products and technologies (labor efficiency factors) with the purpose of receiving economic and social results of industrial and commercial character.

    Changes in the traditional style of thinking and management are associated with the transition to an innovative management model, which implies the presence of staff with developed abilities for innovative activities. Innovation management provides the ideas of complex use of innovations to provide the innovative type of development of natural systems, mastering of competitive advantages in the dynamic market environment.

  • Coordination
    is the function aimed at ensuring coherence in the activities of all connected parts of the organization by establishing rational communications between them.
  • The mission of the management of the organization
    the concept of management, which precedes planning and allows to present the organization as different from others (in its own way unique and inimitable), with its corporate values, management style, technologies of decision making, its deep-rooted traditional culture. The mission should reflect the core values of the innovative business.
  • Innovation management system.
    From the organizational point of view, innovation management is a system consisting of two functionally different subsystems: "material", oriented directly to the production and technological process of the innovative project implementation; "intangible", the object of which are innovations of the organizational and management mechanism (management technology, its structural, financial and commercial schemes, brands, trademark, consumer relations, staff training, etc.).
  • Content of innovation management:
    study of processes of the innovation creation; development of plans (programs) of innovation activity; coordination of activity of all production subdivisions of the organization from the point of view of the system approach; provision of projects (programs) of innovation activity with financial and material resources; equipment of innovation subdivisions with qualified personnel and formation of target groups in them, as a rule, temporary for realization of innovation problems; control over the course of creation of a new program of innovation activity.
  • Strategic management of the organization
    activity on definition of mission, goals of the organization; analysis of external environment, internal potential of the organization and strategic alternatives; strategy evaluation; operative management and planning (budget, goal management); strategy realization; development of the organization structure; personnel selection.
  • Spheres of activity of the organizations in which management innovations are used:
    organizational structure and division of work, organization of work of heads and specialists, control, organization of wages, material and moral stimulation, development of organizational culture, formation of good mood at co-workers. Management innovations can be effectively used in almost all areas of the organization.
  • Requirements imposed to an innovative organization:
    strategic and operational independence, authority to position in the market, the ability to determine the volume of production and range of products, its technological and organizational structure, to ensure the sustainability of its own development.
  • Factors of stability in the innovation organization:
    constant renewal of products; improvement of the structure of their assets (mainly due to their intellectual component); increase in the activity and creative output of the personnel; reduction of all types of costs; concentration of efforts and resources on more promising areas of innovation activity; development of flexible organizational and structural solutions.
  • Functions of innovative management
    the activity characterizing the content of the management process of innovative activity: goal formation, forecasting, planning, organization, control, etc.
