Innovation activity as an object of financing

  • Investment activities
    include market research of the investment market; investing in order to preserve and increase capital; and designing, building or purchasing a completed investment.
  • Classification of sources of financing innovations by types of property.

    Sources of financing are divided into:

    1. state investment resources (budget funds, extra-budgetary funds, state borrowings, shareholdings, state property);
    2. investment resources of economic entities of commercial and non-commercial orientation, as well as public organizations, individuals, etc.;
    3. investment resources of collective investors, as well as investment funds and companies, non-state pension funds, insurance companies, enterprises' own funds, credit resources of commercial banks, other credit institutions and investment banking structures specially authorized by the government.
  • Classification of sources of innovation financing according to the level of management.
    At the level of the state, constituent entities of the Federation and municipalities, the sources of innovation financing are budgets and extra-budgetary funds; borrowed funds from the state credit and banking and insurance systems; borrowed funds in the form of external international borrowings and internal debt in the form of state and other loans.
  • Financial leasing
    is a type of transaction financing used when there is a need for rapid industrial development of major innovations. An entrepreneurial activity aimed at investing temporarily free or borrowed financial resources when, under a leasing (financial lease) agreement, the lessee undertakes to purchase the property stipulated by an agreement from a certain seller and to make the property available to the lessor for a fee for temporary use.
  • Forms of state support for innovation activities:
    direct financing; provision of free bank loans to individual inventors and small innovative enterprises; creation of venture innovation funds that enjoy significant tax benefits; reduction of state patent fees for individual inventors; deferral of payment of patent fees for resource-saving inventions; accelerated depreciation of equipment; creation of a network of technopolises, technoparks, etc.
