The conceptual apparatus. Classification of innovations

  • Basis innovations (major acquisitions)
    the basis for the formation of new generations (trends) of technology, characterized by particularly effective consumer properties, have no known world analogues and have long-term competitive advantages.
  • Innovations in the field of management
    improvement of organizational structure, style and methods of decision making, use of new means of information processing; innovations in planning, motivation; innovations in control; innovations in assessment, training and placement of personnel.
  • Innovations of a product or raw material
    (extending literally to all kinds of products - from toothpaste to steel beams, from computers to glue, from jet aircraft to cars and soybeans) is expressed in any operation that creates new value. Paid services (commercial, medical, hotel and other businesses) are also included.
  • Classification of innovations

    by different signs or functions:

    1. basic;
    2. improving;
    3. rationalizing;
    4. by field of activity: product; production; trade;
    5. increasing product value or creating new value for the consumer: divided into incremental, substantial and breakthrough;
    6. social innovations;
    7. innovations in planning;
    8. motivational innovations;
    9. innovations in control;
    10. innovations in the evaluation, training and placement of management personnel;
    11. innovations in the field of management.
  • Signs of innovations that distinguish them from each other
    technological parameters, novelty, place in the organization, the depth of changes made, the sphere of activity, the most labor-intensive high-tech industries (such as aerospace industry, military industrial complex, pharmaceutical manufacturing, development and creation of electronic and production-communication equipment).
  • Growth innovations
    making a small impact on the increase in net profit of the organization. It increases consumer satisfaction with the product.
  • Product (technological) innovations
    are aimed at creating new products or modernizing existing ones.
  • Manufacturing innovations
    are focused on expanding production capacities and diversifying production activities.
  • Breakthrough innovations
    provide a revolution in a specific area of knowledge and the sphere of their practical application, lead to a revolutionary change of the established concepts, a multiple increase in productivity of labor and profit.
  • Social innovations
    is associated with improving working conditions, social security, psychological climate and internal relations.
  • Strategic innovations
    a review of existing industry methods of creating value for consumers in order to meet newly emerging customer needs, increase the value of products, to form new markets and consumer groups, new approaches to marketing or advertising of products, to implement new methods of sales, improve the system of customer service.
  • Significant innovations
    creates great opportunities to meet the needs of consumers and helps the real growth and development of the organization.
  • Trade innovations
    are focused on the modification of trade activities, pricing policy, offering related services, improving the system and methods of marketing products.
  • Improving innovations
    provide positive changes aimed at partial improvement of outdated generations of technology and technology-related technologies.
