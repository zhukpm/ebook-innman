  • Venture entrepreneurship
    proactive, independent activity of a business entity in a high-risk environment, aimed at developing and bringing a scientific idea (technical invention) to the stage of practical use. It is characterized by great diversity and a wide choice of sources of risk capital. It requires, in addition to financial investment, the formation of demand for the scientific and technical products it produces.
  • Venture capital

    is a source of funding for young, fast-growing industries to develop and bring a scientific idea (invention) to the stage of practical use. One of the properties of venture capital is its growing connection with intellectual labor, new knowledge and intellectual industrial property.

    It is intended for long-term and potentially high-return risk investments. Venture financing takes two main forms: the acquisition of shares in new firms and the provision of loans of various types (with the right of conversion into shares). The main financial purpose of investors is to gain profit by selling their share in the financed company.

  • Subjects of the innovation process:
    organizations producing marketable scientific products, as well as organizations from various branches of the economy that use science-technological developments on knowledge-intensive products.
