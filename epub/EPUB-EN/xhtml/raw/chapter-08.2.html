2. Theories of motivation

To motivate employees of an organization to innovation activity it is necessary to have knowledge and ability to use classical theories of motivation. Theories that are internal and external to the organization are developed.

The internal ones include the theory of needs by A. Maslow and the theory of "labor enrichment" by F. Herzberg, while the external ones include theories that develop systems for monetary incentives for effective work based on the standardization of work and training of personnel in methods of their implementation (a method that was laid down by F. Taylor). In all cases, the recommendations of theories can be used to find appropriate opportunities to stimulate "immeasurable" innovation activity. It is important that success is encouraged and immediately after the work is done.

The most well-known theory, widely used in innovative organizations, is the theory of meeting the needs of A. Maslow. According to this theory, human needs are divided into five categories: - physiological needs (food, clothing, housing); - the need for economic security (high wages that allow you to make savings, health and social insurance, pension programs and job security); - social needs — a sense of belonging to a team, a desire for communication, mutual respect, which are higher in the hierarchy than financial well-being; - status needs — the need for recognition, a sense of the value of one's personality as an integral part of a whole (these needs are closely related to the concept of status, which means the" importance " of a person in the eyes of others); - needs for self-expression — needs of the highest order, when a person works not just to satisfy the financial interest, the significance of their functions and their abilities, but experiencing satisfaction from the very process of work.

  • физиологические потребности (пища, одежда, жилище);
  • потребность в экономической безопасности (высокая заработная плата, позволяющая делать сбережения, медицинское и социальное страхование, программы пенсионного обеспечения и гарантии занятости);
  • социальные потребности—чувство принадлежности к коллективу, желание общения, взаимоуважения, которые стоят по иерархии выше, нежели финансовое благополучие;
  • статусные потребности — потребности в признании, ощущение ценности своей личности как неотъемлемой части единого целого (эти потребности тесно связаны с понятием статуса, которое обозначает «важность» человека в глазах окружающих);
  • потребности в самовыражении — потребности высшего порядка, когда человек трудится не просто для удовлетворения финансового интереса, значимости своих функций и своих способностей, но испытывая удовлетворение от самого процесса труда.

The theory of expectations (expectations of how situations and possible results of actions will be formed in practice) should be singled out in the aggregate of theories of innovation activity motivation. According to this theory, a necessary condition for motivation to achieve a certain goal is (in addition to having an active need) a person's belief that the chosen type of behavior will lead to the satisfaction of the desired. This theory reflects the strong motivation of employees ' behavior at all levels of the management hierarchy.

In the theory of expectations, we find an explanation of the process of choosing a certain way of action (behavior) by a person who is concerned with satisfying any of his needs, and in the conditions of some quite logical assumptions (differences in needs cause differences in assessments; people consciously choose their way of action; the choice is made in the hope of turning the actions performed into the desired result).

The expectation of remuneration is a powerful motivator for purposeful activation of employee behavior. Although the theory of expectations has a long history, its relatively new modification, proposed by V. Vroom, has become the most widespread.

According to V. Vroom's model of expectations, the chain of the following factors is crucial for motivation to work: labor costs, results, and, finally, remuneration and valence (satisfaction with the reward). The final factor in the expectation theory is the valence (reward value), which characterizes the expected level of relative satisfaction or dissatisfaction that occurs in connection with the reward.

The theory of expectations has a slightly different interpretation from the point of view of its motivational capabilities. In this respect, the modified theory of motivation proposed by D. Nadler and E. Lawler is indicative. According to their understanding of expectation theory, the amount of labor spent on a particular task depends on the expected reward. Moreover, employees take into account the following factors: - how well are they able to cope with a certain task (“Can I do this?”); - will they get the expected reward for the work they do ("If I do it, how much will I get?"); - does such a reward correspond to the work spent (“Is it worth the effort?”).

  • насколько они способны справиться с определенной задачей («Могу ли я это сделать?»);
  • получат ли они ожидаемое вознаграждение за выполненную работу («Если я ее выполню, сколько я получу?»);
  • соответствует ли такое вознаграждение затраченному труду («Стоит ли прилагать усилия?»).

The essence of this theory is that it considers differences in labor motivation from the point of view of individual characteristics of a person: each employee perceives the difficulty of the task, the "value" of remuneration and their relationship in their own way. Hence, it is possible to achieve an increase in labor efficiency by calculating the amount of remuneration that satisfies each employee, determining the desired and, moreover, achievable level of his productivity, establishing the relationship between remuneration and labor productivity and ensuring a sufficient amount of this remuneration.

The theory of expectations was further developed in the works of W. Newsem, who enriched it with a number of significant factors. This theory summarizes some of the practical recommendations that have been formulated by managers who have been successful in applying them. Let's recite these factors: Ability. Is this individual able to do a certain job well? Confidence. Is he confident he will be able to perform it well? Complexity. Will he have to spend a lot of effort to do it well? Criteria. Does he understand the difference between good and bad work? Trust. Does he believe that the Manager will deliver on his promises (that everyone will receive equally desirable remuneration for good work and equally less desirable remuneration for bad work)? Compensation. Does the reward correspond to good performance? Expenses. What does a good job cost an individual: what is it "cheaper " to give up — effort or reward? Interaction. Does the manager make contact with employees? (the main component of the entire process).

  • Способность. Способен ли данный индивид хорошо выполнять определенную работу?
  • Уверенность. Уверен ли он, что сможет выполнить ее хорошо?
  • Сложность. Придется ли ему затратить много усилий, чтобы хорошо ее выполнить?
  • Критерии. Понимает ли он разницу между хорошей и плохой работой?
  • Доверие. Верит ли он, что менеджер выполнит свои обещания (что все получат одинаково желательное вознаграждение за хорошую работу и одинаково менее желательное вознаграждение за плохую)?
  • Компенсация. Соответствует ли вознаграждение хорошим результатам работы?
  • Издержки. Что стоит индивиду хорошая работа: от чего «дешевле» отказаться — от усилий или от вознаграждения?
  • Взаимодействие. Вступает ли менеджер в контакты со служащими? (главная составляющая всего процесса).

Expectations are based on the employee's perceptions of what they think is most important to them. Certain activity in this field is very diverse. There are popular attempts to develop such labor performance criteria, in particular for managers, that allow them to more or less objectively assess their contribution to achieving the expected final results in the long term.

When creating a system of remuneration according to the expectations of employees, it is important to take into account not only their individual qualities, but also their position in the management hierarchy. Thus, at the lower levels of management, managers are encouraged primarily for their ability to make independent decisions, for independence and sharpness of judgment, for perseverance and even aggressiveness in implementing decisions. If a young Manager achieves significant results and gets promoted in a short period of time, then he naturally believes that these qualities are encouraged by the management.

However, at higher levels, other qualities and skills are needed: the ability to take into account and analyze the opinions of subordinates, focus on long-term goals rather than short-term tasks, create a team and encourage it to work hard. The potential effectiveness of remuneration that does not adequately take into account the nature of expectations inevitably remains largely unclaimed.

Differences in individual groups of needs that can change the expected motivational orientation of the employee can be grouped according to the following characteristics (motives): achievements, inter-relationships, power.

the motive of achievement encourages to achieve the highest results of activity, the motive of inter-relationships reflects the need to be together with other people, the motive of power-the desire to exert influence.

The leading sign of the manifestation of the motive of achievement is the desire to advance, grow, taking into account the results of their activities, values, etc. a sign of the manifestation of the inter-relationship motive is the desire to maintain good relationships in the team. When the power motive dominates, a person wants to force others to act in a way that they would not act without compulsion (the power motive is inherent in most managers, especially successful ones).

As you know, the types of people considered clearly differ from each other. Therefore, an employee with a single pronounced motive does not fit into a position that requires a completely different motive (behavior).

For practical purposes, lists of signs of the manifestation of the main motives and the most appropriate ways to stimulate the work of employees can be proposed.

In a situation where the achievement motive dominates, the employee's behavior is characterized by the following features: - constant need for information about the results of their activities (a clear need for evaluation); - desire for individual tasks and responsibility; - moderate risk appetite; - concern for work results rather than methods; - comparing own current results (achievements) with the past, but not with the achievements of other people; - setting high goals for self; - search for new solutions; - unwillingness to work in a group (works poorly); - constant tension; - little concern for working conditions; - internal focus on tasks, work; - insensitivity and unresponsiveness in relationships with people.

  • постоянной потребностью в информации о результатах своей деятельности (яркая потребность в оценке);
  • желанием индивидуальных заданий и ответственности;
  • склонностью к риску, но умеренному;
  • заботой о результатах работы, нежели о методах;
  • сопоставлением своих настоящих результатов (достижений) с прошлыми, но не с достижениями других людей;
  • постановкой себе высоких целей;
  • поиском новых решений;
  • нежеланием работать в группе (работает плохо);
  • постоянной напряженностью;
  • малой заботой об условиях труда;
  • внутренней ориентированностью на задачи, труд;
  • нечуткостью и неотзывчивостью во взаимоотношениях с людьми.

The preferred ways to encourage employees of the above type are: - assignment of specific tasks with clearly evaluated results and at the same time associated with risk; - periodically changing tasks to more complex ones; - granting the right to make decisions, and assigning responsibility for completing complex tasks; - clear definition of the terms of the task and the employee's role, so that it is clear to both him and others; - providing access to resources, information, and so on; - protection from impact of factors that may hinder individual development.

  • поручение конкретных заданий с четко оцениваемыми результатами и притом связанных с риском;
  • периодическая смена заданий на все более сложные;
  • предоставление права принимать решения, и возложение ответственности за выполнение сложных заданий;
  • четкое определение роли работника, чтобы она была ясна как ему самому, так и окружающим, и терминов исполнения задания;
  • предоставление доступа к ресурсам, информации и т. д.;
  • защита от воздействия факторов, которые могут препятствовать индивидуальному развитию.

The distinctive features of such an employee include: ability to work always and everywhere; a wide range of suitable tasks; great vigor and initiative; constant lack of time; always knowing how to complete the assigned task; transposition of working hours and leisure; inability to rest; desire to surpass everyone (supported by effort).

In a situation where the interrelationships motive dominates, the employee's behavior has its own distinctive features. Such employee: - perceives information about the results of work as information about his personality. He strives to get personal recognition, to have many good comrades; - tries to create loyal relationships, be loyal to his organization and be its defender, avoid leadership positions and leadership role; - makes amends for conflicts, tries to maintain contacts with everyone, cares about employees, is responsive in relationships with people.

  • воспринимает информацию о результатах труда как информацию о своей личности. Он стремится получить персональное признание, иметь много хороших товарищей;
  • старается создавать лояльные отношения, быть преданным своей организации и ее защитником, избегает руководящей должности и роли лидера;
  • заглаживает конфликты, старается поддерживать контакты со всеми, заботится о сотрудниках, отзывчив во взаимоотношениях с людьми.

When motivating an employee with expectations of this kind, it is desirable: - encourage their participation in meetings and other events; - publicly celebrate and encourage them for their success, and if possible try not to criticize or punish them for their omissions; - to include in the various time groups; - resort to methods of collective influence; - apply rotation (horizontal transposition); - give him the opportunity to become an integrator in some group; - to get rid of the factors that prevent him from working in a group and contacting employees.

  • поощрять его участие в совещаниях и иных мер оприятиях;
  • публично отмечать и стимулировать его за успехи, по возможности стараться не критиковать и не наказывать за упущения;
  • включать в различные временные группы;
  • прибегать к методам коллективного воздействия;
  • применять ротацию (горизонтальное перемещение);
  • предоставить ему возможность стать интегратором в какой-нибудь группе;
  • избавлять его от факторов, мешающих работать в группе и контактировать с сотрудниками.

The behavior of an employee who is dominated by the motive of power is characterized by the following features: - the desire to help and stimulate; - the manifestation of will and power in relationships; - interest in connections that are useful for fixing its status and prestige; - the skill to convince; - comparing the results achieved with the results of other employees.

  • желанием помочь и стимулировать;
  • проявлением воли и власти во взаимоотношениях;
  • заинтересованностью в связях, полезных для закрепления его статуса и престижа;
  • умением убеждать;
  • сравнением достигнутых результатов с результатами других сотрудников.

Employees of this type should be create conditions that allow them to meet their expectations by: - providing an opportunity to participate in all meetings where the company's strategic goals are discussed or important decisions are made; - assignment as a project manager, appointment as an expert; - showing concern for the growth of his status, rewarding with titles, signs, etc. (symbols noticed by each); - demonstrating that he is considered an equal partner; - involvement in the solution of "particularly important" tasks (for example, designing new products, reorganizing the organizational structure, etc.).

  • предоставления возможности участвовать во всех совещаниях, где обсуждаются стратегические цели предприятия, либо принимаются важные решения;
  • поручения руководства проектом, назначения экспертом;
  • проявления заботы о росте его статуса, вознаграждения титулами, знаками и т. п. (замечаемыми каждому символами);
  • демонстрирования того, что его считают равноценным партнером;
  • привлечения к решению «особо важных» заданий (например, проектирование новой продукции, реорганизация оргструктуры и т. п.).

However, employees are different and therefore it is important to have reliable information about the reasons that determine the lower and irrational expectations of employees, characteristic of truants, alcoholics, and the low-cultured. in this regard, we will name several circumstances in the presence of which expectations and, accordingly, motivation does not bring the intended effect-a weak motive for achievement, lack of need for individual development, low level of qualification and education, unsatisfactory external factors of the labor process (low wages, poor working conditions, etc.).

In eliminating such shortcomings, the importance of enriching the work of employees, taking into account their level of education and typological characteristics, is important. In all circumstances, the impulses that affect the content of work and professional activity should come to the fore.

To strengthen the potential expectations of senior managers, awareness of current and future tasks (which directly concern them), as well as a clear definition of their roles, responsibilities, rights, responsibilities, etc. can serve as serious motivators. Otherwise, the manager does not feel firm in his endeavors, may make erroneous decisions. He has the right to have a clear understanding of the boundaries of his competence and role in the management system.

It should be remembered that the main sources of motivation are freedom of choice, the right to decide, and independence. The possibility of choice is guaranteed only when the role function of the employee is clearly defined. Greater freedom of choice is characteristic of the teams of successful enterprises, which are characterized by common value orientations, an atmosphere of mutual understanding and friendliness. In their daily activities, they follow the rules of ethical behavior shared by all employees.
