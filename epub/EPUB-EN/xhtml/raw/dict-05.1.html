  • Organizational forms of innovative management:
    specialized business organizations or the divisions various for the reason of an extreme variety of objects (processes) of innovations. They include: central services on mastering of new production; project-target groups on carrying out of scientific researches, development and manufacturing of production; departments of research and development; venture departments; specialized centralized funds of stimulation of innovations; consulting and analytical groups; temporary target groups for complex decision of innovative problems; venture groups; engineering firms.
  • Tendencies of development the organizational structures of innovative type:
    transition from narrow functional specialization to integration; debureaucratization; reduction the number of hierarchical levels (transformation of organizational structures from pyramidal to flat, with a minimum number of levels between the top management and direct executives); domination the through management of innovative process (from origin of idea to its realization).
  • Forms of scientific and technical cooperation in innovative activity between organizations:
    Joint scientific research and design development with the aim to solve certain scientific, technical and production problems (concerning, for example, cooperation on a commercial basis to reduce costs or increase product quality); joint ventures to implement some scientific, technical or production cooperation program by common efforts; industrial cooperation in the field of industrial and technical relations between independent organizations; franchising; leasing; engineering; licensing trade, etc.
  • Franchising
    a form of contractual relations arising between independent organizations on the basis of trade in goods, technology or services. In accordance with the franchising agreement, the seller (franchisor) provides to the buyer the right to use its trademark, trade name and other objects of intellectual property.
  • Leasing
    forms of machinery and equipment rent, when the lessor provides the lessee with the goods for exclusive use for a specified period of time for a certain fee on the basis of a leasing agreement. The lessor retains ownership of the goods. The lessee has a temporary right to use the product. The agreement provides for rendering permanent technical assistance to the lessee by the lessor (in the form of maintenance and consultation).
