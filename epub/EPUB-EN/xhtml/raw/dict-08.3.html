  • Motivation of innovative organization:
    efficiency (profitability); creativity (aspiration to increase knowledge, implementation of creative ideas); satisfaction of consumers' demands, formation of new needs; prestige; satisfaction of special needs. All the variety of complementary motives should be focused on specific innovations, the carriers of which are creative workers associated with the technical preparation of the production process, experimental and main production.
  • Principles of personnel motivation:
    distribution of work taking into account professional and socio-psychological qualities of employees; urgency of encouragement, quick and fair reaction to actions of employees; stimulation by intermediate results. For this purpose, the overall task should be divided and planned by stages so that each of them could be adequately assessed and duly rewarded; unexpectedness of incentives. Unpredictable and irregular incentives motivate more than predictable ones, when they practically become part of the wage. Fairness is a universal motive for human behavior, which also applies to the systems that motivate innovation.
