  • Intellectual property:
    the exclusive right of an individual or a legal entity to the results of intellectual activity and similar means of individualization of a legal entity, individualization of products, works or services. As applied to the field of innovation economy, it includes the right to: inventions in all fields of human activity; hypotheses (concepts); scientific discoveries; scientific and design developments; industrial designs; trademarks, service marks, trade names and commercial designations; protection against unfair competition and rights relating to intellectual activity, including in the production field.
  • Patent
    a document certifying authorship and giving its owner the exclusive right to an invention (no one can use the invention without the consent of the patent owner). It is the owner's title to an invention, which is supported by a design or trademark registration. Consent to the use of the invention is expressed by granting (selling) a license for partial use or full transfer of patent rights.
