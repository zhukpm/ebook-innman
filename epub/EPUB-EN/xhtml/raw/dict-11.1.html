The concept of breakthrough technologies

  • The basic source of breakthrough technologies:
    culture, the values derived from it and the accumulated knowledge of mankind, enabling the state to create special conditions conducive to the formation and realization of breakthrough technologies.
  • Classification of breakthrough technologies:
    grouping technologies according to the chosen criterion. All breakthrough technologies can be roughly divided into two groups: formative and disruptive. Breakthrough technologies can be critical, dual-use, and disruptive.
  • Critical technologies:
    Technologies that are of paramount importance for the development of the national economy and are most important from the point of view of public needs and interests. It is possible to allocate them in each of priority directions of development of a science and engineering. They have an interbranch character and create essential preconditions for development of many technological areas. They include those technical artefacts, projects and technologies, the creation of which could radically affect improvement of quality of life, improvement of health and solution of social problems. Technologies of radical commercial and social importance include: communication technologies, continental and trans-continental transport systems, energy technologies, technologies of environmental nature, as well as those affecting public health and demographic dynamics, and social technologies related to megacity management and the agro-industrial complex.
  • Dual-use technologies:
    civil-military technologies. The best military technologies are given to the civil sector of economy with the purpose of production of the newest consumer goods and services on industrial scale. This is the essence of these technologies, defining their value.
  • Closing technologies:
    Technologies whose emergence and application make a huge number of existing technologies redundant and obsolete. Closing energy- and resource-saving breakthrough technologies are capable to make revolutionary changes in economy of the country. They are product of collective creativity and, as a rule, require high-class specialists for their introduction.
  • Breakthrough technologies basis:
    concentration of intellectual potential of specialists from different fields and branches of science, engineering and production on the most priority scientific and technical directions; new physical principles, unconventional ways and technical solutions of actual scientific and technical problems of economy and security of the country; highly efficient organizational and methodological forms of interaction between structural subdivisions of the organization and external partners, including research and production and other teams of the country.
  • Main directions of scientific and technical activity of the state in creation of breakthrough dual-use technologies:
    laser, nuclear thermonuclear, plasma physics, rocket-space, optoelectronic, microwave, information, communication, unconventional energy, construction, medical and preventive, training, diagnostic, agro-industrial.
  • Priority areas for federal-level critical technologies:
    information technology and electronics, manufacturing technology, new materials and chemical products, fuels and energy.
  • Breakthrough technology:
    a concentration of diverse knowledge energy that, under certain conditions, 'explodes' the conventional (traditional) understanding of an object, opening up space for fundamentally new possibilities of its qualitative transformation. It allows an organization to achieve impressive results in a short period of time, and to be significantly ahead of its competitors. It is a product of national innovation system and innovation management, an important factor of economic growth and social development. It includes a set of production processes, scientific description of production methods or management methods that have no analogues in the world and lead to revolutionary changes in various spheres of human life.
  • Breakthrough innovation:
    an innovation (product, service or development strategy change) that leads to a significant increase in the organization’s revenue and net profit.
  • Breakthrough disruptive technologies:
    technologies that ignore cultural values, are in conflict with them and are oriented towards making super-profits.
  • Systematic breakthrough technologies:
    technologies that are in line with, and not in conflict with, the common cultural values of civilization. They are born at the crossroads of peoples cultures and are based on the innovative achievements of these cultures. They serve as an important basis for the development and survival of nations.
