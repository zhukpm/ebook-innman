  • State innovation policy in science and technology
    is the implementation of major innovation processes aimed at creating breakthrough technologies of state importance. It is an integral part of the state's social and economic policy, which presents its vision, ideology, goals and objectives for innovation. It is focused on solving innovative problems of intersectoral and sectoral nature that lead to a radical change in the country's technological base and require concentration of resources on the national economy scale. It expresses the attitude of the state authorities to innovation activity and its results.
  • State regulation of innovative activity
    the activity which is carried out for the purpose of maintenance of progressive transformations in sphere of material production and increase of competitiveness of a national product by strengthening of positions of home producers in the world markets of the goods and services.
  • Indirect regulation of the innovation activities of organizations
    is carried out without direct government involvement. The development of science and technology and the mastering of their achievements in the public and private sectors of the economy is regulated by tax, depreciation, patent and customs policies, as well as by supporting innovative small enterprises, providing various benefits to the subjects of the innovation process; creating an innovation climate in the economy, the development of infrastructure for research and development, including services for the preparation of forecasts of scientific and technological development and the formation of a system of national scientific priorities on their basis.
  • Requirements to the state innovative activity
    other clearly established objectives, management structure, means of regulation and support through which the state authorities influence the organizations and environment of their functioning; information system required to replenish (support) the state functions of innovative activity.
  • Federal innovation policy
    is to create a favorable innovation climate for the implementation of state priorities of scientific and technical development in the economic sphere. At the national level, the innovation policy includes: initiation of innovation programs and projects aimed at the implementation of scientific and technical innovations; transfer of new technologies from the public sector to the real sector of economy; creation of the innovation market and its infrastructure; conducting research and development in accordance with the system of state scientific priorities; legal regulation of the state patent policy and standardization policy.
