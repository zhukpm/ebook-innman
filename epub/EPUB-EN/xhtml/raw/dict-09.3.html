Chapter 9. Innovation culture

The role of the manager in developing an innovation culture

  • Indicators of the cultural-psychological environment of innovation:
    type of leadership and leadership style; socio-psychological climate; delegation and competitiveness; satisfaction of material demands; past experience with innovation; level of competence of senior managers sufficient to guarantee compliance with the culture and the chosen strategy.
  • Prerequisites for a cultural and psychological environment for innovation:
    flexible organizational structures; an acceptable level of bureaucratization of management; a high level of organizational culture; innovation orientation; flexible organizational forms of innovation management; availability of modern experimental facilities; advisory support.
  • Requirements for the manager of an innovative organization:
    openness and commitment to innovative ideas; support for creativity; facilitate adaptation to changing conditions; ensure that the organization’s strategy is aligned with the long-term interests of all those affected by it (customers, suppliers, employees, shareholders, etc.); ability to motivate the initiators and agents of effective change for the future; awareness of the fit between strategy and culture; communicating with the organization’s staff about the meaning and usefulness of the changes being implemented (not only their appropriateness but also their possibility).
