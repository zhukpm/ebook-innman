  • Innovation forecasting
    assessment and choice of acceptable managerial decisions among several variants of development of the organization. These variants should present indicators used for development of strategic plans, defining quality of products and services, carrying out research and development work. In the forecast the innovative tasks on all structure of control system are developed and estimated. Innovations are predicted mainly with the help of methods of expert evaluations and modeling. Forecasts differ in: time of action - short-term (up to one year), medium-term (up to five years) and long-term (more than 20 years); scale of action - global, national, regional; types of innovation activities - product, process, strategic.
  • Criteria and indicators for evaluation and selection of innovative projects.
    Financial and economic criteria and corresponding indicators are mainly used. If necessary, when assessing qualitative parameters, characteristics are analyzed, as a rule, not having a quantitative assessment, but significantly supplementing the ideas about the activity of innovative organization. In addition to financial and economic criteria, there are normative criteria, criteria of resource provision, and strategic criteria. As indicators there are: the cost of the project; profitability, calculated on the basis of capital investments; payback period; efficiency factor; savings on conditionally constant expenses and others.
  • Principles of innovative activity:
    priority that supposes necessity of inclusion in the plan of innovations, perspective of which is confirmed by forecasts, calculations and which realization provides considerable economic and social benefits not only in short-term, but also in medium-term and long-term periods; continuity that obliges to consecutive working out short-term, medium-term and long-term plans of innovative development; systematicity - through planning on all stages of life cycle of innovation subject, especially in large organizations, and Complexity - close interrelation of the innovative plan with adequate sections of the plan of economic and social development of the organization, the industrial program, investment and financial plans, personnel policy of the organization; economic substantiation - inclusion in the plan of only economically substantiated actions provided with necessary resources presented in the business plan of the organization.
  • The system of programs and projects
    interconnected elements of the subsystems that form the basis of the program management method. In turn, the main form of program management is targeted comprehensive programs based on the principles of program-targeted planning.
