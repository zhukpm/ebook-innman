  • Analysis of the problem situation:
    a research method that helps to identify the causes of the situation; determine the degree of its novelty, the relationship with other problems; establish measures of solvability of the problem, especially from the perspective of information and resource provision. Various methods are used to analyze the situation and select options. The most widespread are methods of mathematical statistics, game theory, probability theory, linear and non-linear programming, simulation models, graph theory, queues, mass maintenance. It is important to ensure the proper balance in the use of modern analytical methods and subjective judgments in the practice of making innovative decisions.
  • Types and models of innovative solutions:
    intuitive, heuristic, approximate, hypothetical individual, local, recurring, predictive, prospective, global. Types and models of innovative decisions are various, difficult and dynamic. These solutions should be made mainly in conditions of uncertainty and risk. In innovative management types and models of the made decisions depend on stages of life cycle of innovations.
  • Problem situation:
    a situation in which there is a discrepancy between the organization’s desired position and the actual position, for the resolution of which a targeted solution is developed and implemented in a timely manner, involving the involvement and use of necessary resources.
  • Scheme of innovative solution development and implementation:
    decision-making procedure including anticipation of innovation; planning of innovation; analysis of the problem situation; identification of the needs for innovation; clarification of objectives; definition of criteria for choosing solution options; development of options; selection of the best solution option; organization of solution implementation; control and evaluation of results.
