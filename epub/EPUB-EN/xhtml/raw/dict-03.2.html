  • Innovation activity
    the activity based on creation, development, dissemination and application of scientific and technical knowledge in all areas of science and technology. As a result, productive forces and organizational-economical relations in economic systems are transformed. Innovation activity itself is an innovative process - the process of transformation of scientific and technical knowledge into innovations when in a chain of events starting with a new idea, it is matured, embodied in a concrete product, technology or service form and further spreads.
  • Types of innovation activity
    are countless and represent in every evolving system. The main types of innovation activities can be grouped as follows: preparation and organization of production; modification of the introduced product and technologies corresponding to the training of personnel; production design; acquisition of unknowns (patents, licenses, "know-how", brands, trademarks and other services); marketing of innovation processes.
  • Innovation process
    a process that is focused on the creation of a new product, technology (discovery, invention and in general "know-how"), or activities, the final result of which is the commercial implementation of a new product (technology, product, materials), technology, including new methods of organization and production management. The innovation process does not end with the introduction (appearance on the market) of a new product and technology. As the innovation spreads, it continues to improve, discover previously unknown properties and becomes more and more effective.
  • Instruments of innovation process regulation
    state and administrative, general economic and market regulators; socio-economic and scientific and technical forecasts of the state policy; national and regional innovation systems; mechanisms of state regulation of scientific and technical and innovation activity; methods of complex resource, including the number of investment, maintenance of scientific and technical and innovation activity; forms and processes of constant renewal of human resources, intelligence
  • Phases (stages) of the innovation process:
    systematization of incoming ideas; study and evaluation of the created innovation subject; selection of revealed ideas, development of a concept and creation of a new product; market testing of a new product and final production substantiation of its implementation; introduction of the product into practice; study and evaluation of the results of implementation with the aim of further development of the innovation subject.
