  • Civil society

    a set of formal and non-formal organizations and rules (institutions) that connect an individual or family (household) with the state (government) and business (private sector).

    It occupies positions between the state and the household. It represented by organized groups and associations, separated from the state, having a certain autonomy in relations with it, is voluntary formed by the members of society to protect, support and spread its values, to provide the balance of interests with business and power.

  • Public consciousness

    a holistic spiritual phenomenon, which has an internal structure that includes different levels and forms of consciousness.

    It actively influences the being, the whole social life. It serves as one of the necessary factors and conditions for solving social problems. The power of influence of public consciousness depends on completeness and accuracy of reflection of real regularities and needs of social development, on particular conditions and objective possibilities of changing social being, on connection of consciousness with spirituality and practical activity.

  • The public-power sphere

    includes the legislature, executive and judicial branches of state power, their organization in regions - subjects of the Federation, as well as legislative and executive bodies of local self-government. In other words, the public-power sphere is personified in the state bodies, municipal government.

    It is designed to guarantee the balance of interests of the subjects of industrial-economics, socio-political and public-power activities, as well as to provide general conditions for effective functionalization and development of all major spheres of society.

  • Socio-political sphere
    represented by institutions of civic society. It carries out function of consolidation, declaring and following lobbying of interests of professional, demographic, national, confessional, social, political and other structures of the society organization.
  • Structural elements of civil society in the economic sphere
    self-organizing structures of entrepreneurs and employers (associations and unions of entrepreneurs), consumers (unions of consumers, consumer cooperatives and their associations), trade unions, other voluntary associations of citizens, created by them on their own initiative in order to consolidate and lobby their specific social and economic interests.
  • Conditions of functioning of the civil society
    presence of the developed, various social structure reflecting richness and variety of interests of representatives of various groups and layers; possession by each member of a society of the concrete property or participation in possession of it, the right to use it and to dispose of it at own discretion; high level of social, intellectual, psychological development of the person, its internal freedom and ability to full independence at inclusion in one or another public institution.
  • Functions of civil society
    representing of the citizens interests; involvement of citizens in social processes; provision of social services.
