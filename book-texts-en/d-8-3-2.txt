Principles of personnel motivation. distribution of work taking into account professional and socio-psychological qualities of employees; urgency of encouragement, quick and fair reaction to actions of employees; stimulation by intermediate results. For this purpose, the overall task should be divided and planned by stages so that each of them could be adequately assessed and duly rewarded; unexpectedness of incentives. Unpredictable and irregular incentives motivate more than predictable ones, when they practically become part of the wage. Fairness is a universal motive for human behavior, which also applies to the systems that motivate innovation.
