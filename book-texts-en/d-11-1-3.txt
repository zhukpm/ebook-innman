Critical technologies. Technologies that are of paramount importance for the development of the national economy and are most important from the point of view of public needs and interests. It is possible to allocate them in each of priority directions of development of a science and engineering. They have an interbranch character and create essential preconditions for development of many technological areas. They include those technical artefacts, projects and technologies, the creation of which could radically affect improvement of quality of life, improvement of health and solution of social problems. Technologies of radical commercial and social importance include: communication technologies, continental and trans-continental transport systems, energy technologies, technologies of environmental nature, as well as those affecting public health and demographic dynamics, and social technologies related to megacity management and the agro-industrial complex.
